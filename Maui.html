🌀 Kona Storm — Flood & Wind Watch

Monitoring

A Kona storm system is affecting Maui County with prolonged heavy rainfall, elevated flash flood risk in valleys and stream corridors, strong and gusty winds, and rough coastal seas. Wildfire monitoring remains active as a primary signal and will escalate immediately if NASA FIRMS confirms a hotspot.

⚠ This is situational awareness only. Follow Maui Emergency Management Agency, National Weather Service Honolulu, and county instructions at all times. In an emergency, call 911.

Maui Local Context

Area-specific conditions

🛣
Hāna Highway — Gulch Flooding Risk

Multiple stream crossings along Hāna Highway are susceptible to rapid flooding during heavy Kona rain events. Bridge and road flooding can develop quickly with little visible warning from upstream rainfall.

⚠ Road caution advised
🏔
Windward / Leeward Contrast

Kona storm patterns reverse typical conditions — rain pushes into normally dry West Maui and leeward areas. Standard fire weather patterns do not apply. Flood risk exists across both windward and leeward slopes.

⚠ Atypical rain distribution
🌊
West Maui Coastal Roads

Coastal road segments near Lahaina and Kapalua may experience wave wash and reduced visibility during high surf. Avoid low coastal crossings when seas are elevated.

⚠ Shore approach caution
🏠
Emergency Readiness

Keep emergency supplies accessible. Know your nearest shelter location. Do not wait for an official warning to prepare — monitoring conditions and being ready is the key preparation step.

⚡
Post-Storm Fire Watch

After the storm passes and trade winds return, rapid fuel drying may increase fire risk — especially in West Maui and low-elevation grasslands. Kahu Ola wildfire monitoring remains active through this transition.

🌿 Kūpuna, Keiki & Community Safety

Children, elderly residents, and people with respiratory or mobility conditions need extra care during Kona storm conditions on Maui.

Avoid unnecessary travel during heavy rain. Flash flooding can develop rapidly in valleys and stream corridors.
Do not attempt to cross flooded roadways. Even shallow moving water is dangerous for vehicles and pedestrians.
Check on elderly neighbors and those who may have limited access to emergency alerts.
Keep medications, documents, and emergency supplies ready in case evacuation is ordered.
Monitor air quality after storm — smoke from post-storm fires affects keiki and kūpuna first.

"E mālama kākou i kēlā me kēia." — Let us care for one another. 🌺
