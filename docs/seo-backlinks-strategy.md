# Kahu Ola — Backlinks Strategy & Outreach Templates

**Last updated:** 2026-05-15
**Phase:** C.2 (Backlinks strategy — long-term outreach work)
**Status:** Deliverable. Outreach is manual, executed by operator over months.

This document is a tactical playbook. Backlinks are the strongest off-page
SEO signal Google uses, and they take months to accrue. The realistic
horizon for measurable impact is **6–12 months**.

---

## Strategy summary

The fastest, lowest-effort path to credibility for an independent civic
platform is:

1. **Easy wins first** (Tier 3 — civic tech submission directories). These
   are submission-based and almost always accept relevant projects. Land
   3–5 backlinks here in the first month.
2. **Then governmental .gov/.edu outreach** (Tier 1). Slower, lower hit
   rate, but each landed backlink is worth ~10× a Tier 3 one for SEO.
3. **Then journalism** (Tier 2). News-cycle dependent. Pitch when there's
   a Hawaiʻi disaster news hook (sad, but timing matters).
4. **National tech press** (Tier 4) is a stretch goal. Don't lead with it.

## Measurement

Track in this order, monthly:

1. **Google Search Console → Links report** — authoritative source of who
   actually links to kahuola.org. Free.
2. **Domain authority** via Ahrefs / Moz / Semrush free tier — directional
   only, useful for trend monitoring.
3. **Referring domain count** — quality matters more than quantity. One
   .gov backlink is worth ~10 generic backlinks.

Targets:
- **6 months**: 10+ quality backlinks across all tiers
- **12 months stretch**: 1+ .gov or .edu backlink

## Common pitfalls to avoid

- **Don't pay for backlinks.** Google penalizes paid links and many
  "backlink services" produce spam-graveyard backlinks that can hurt.
- **Don't pitch generic mass emails.** Each Tier 1 / Tier 2 email
  should reference something specific about the recipient's work.
- **Don't over-follow-up.** One polite follow-up at 3–4 weeks is the
  ceiling. More than that and you damage the relationship.
- **Don't exaggerate.** Claims like "leading hazard platform" or "used
  by thousands" without evidence damage credibility immediately. Stick
  to verifiable facts: independent, free, post-Lahaina, etc.
- **Don't ignore the disclaimer requirement.** Every outreach must make
  clear that Kahu Ola does NOT replace official emergency services.
  Misrepresentation here would undo any Tier 1 trust permanently.
