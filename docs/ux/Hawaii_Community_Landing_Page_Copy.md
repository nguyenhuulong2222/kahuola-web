WHY KAHU OLA EXISTS

In August 2023, the Lahaina wildfire showed how difficult it can be for communities to quickly understand environmental risks.

Critical hazard information exists across many scientific systems:

satellite fire detection

weather alerts

air quality monitoring

ocean conditions

Kahu Ola brings these signals together into a single civic dashboard designed for Hawaiʻi residents.

HOW THE SYSTEM WORKS

Kahu Ola translates scientific data into clear signals:

Satellite detections
Weather alerts
Environmental sensors

These signals are processed through a hazard intelligence pipeline and presented in a simple dashboard designed for fast understanding.

Users should be able to understand the hazard situation within five seconds.

DATA SOURCES

Kahu Ola integrates public data from:

NASA FIRMS — wildfire satellite detection
NOAA — radar, weather, and ocean conditions
National Weather Service — alerts and warnings
EPA AirNow — air quality monitoring
USGS — volcanic activity

All signals clearly show their source and update time.

COMMUNITY FOCUS

Kahu Ola is designed for Hawaiʻi communities.

Special attention is given to:

Kūpuna (elders)
Keiki (children)
Families and community groups

The platform prioritizes simple language and mobile accessibility.

IMPORTANT NOTICE

Kahu Ola provides situational awareness using scientific data sources.

Kahu Ola is not an official emergency service.

For evacuation orders and official instructions please follow:

Hawaiʻi Emergency Management Agency (HIEMA)
County Emergency Management offices
National Weather Service
