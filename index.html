How it works

Trusted public hazard data translated fast for Hawaiʻi

Kahu Ola connects multiple public data systems and translates them into plain-language civic signals. During the current Kona storm, flood and wind context rises — but FireSignal remains the primary monitored layer.

01
🛰

Government sensors detect

NASA FIRMS satellites orbit every 90 minutes. NOAA radar scans continuously. NWS meteorologists issue watches and warnings. These systems never stop.

NASA · NOAA · NWS · EPA · USGS · NIFC

02
⚙️

Aggregator normalizes

The Kahu Ola Aggregator ingests, validates, and caches every signal server-side. Raw government data becomes clean, versioned FireSignal · SmokeSignal · Perimeter. Keys never reach the client.

Via /api/hazards/* · keys server-side only

03
📱

You see plain language

The app displays honest, timestamped signals with clear freshness labels. Stale data is always marked. Estimated perimeters are never labeled official. Your location never leaves your device.

Our mission

Built after Lahaina. Built for Hawaiʻi events that change fast.

The 2023 Lahaina wildfire revealed a devastating gap: advanced sensors were tracking danger while residents still lacked calm, plain-language public understanding. That lesson still matters during a statewide Kona storm, when flood, wind, and rough-sea context change quickly.

Kahu Ola was built to close that gap. Not to replace official emergency services — but to translate trusted public data into a civic signal layer that people can understand in seconds before opening official alerts and emergency guidance.

🔥 Wildfire-First 🔒 Privacy-First ⚡ Failure-Tolerant 🏛 Grant-Ready
Engineered for resilience

Cache-first architecture with validated data snapshots. The app renders under 8 distinct failure conditions — including full network loss. A degraded signal is infinitely safer than a blank screen.

Privacy as a design principle

Safety should not require surveillance. Proximity awareness is computed on-device. No location history is built, stored, or transmitted — ever. No accounts. No ads. No data selling.

Honest about uncertainty

Every signal shows its source and age. Estimated fire perimeters are never labeled official. Stale data is always marked. Kahu Ola never fabricates or inflates hazard severity.

Community-driven · Free public service

An independent civic technology initiative — not a government agency, not a commercial product. Built to remain freely accessible statewide, even during high-risk weather events and degraded network conditions.

Privacy-first architecture

Zero PII. Zero tracking.

We do not need to know who you are to help you stay safe. Kahu Ola is designed around a single rule: your data stays on your device.

🚫
No accounts required

Core safety views are accessible without sign-in. No email. No phone number. No profile.

📍
On-device proximity

Hazard distance checks compute locally on your device. Your GPS coordinates never cross the device boundary.

🛡
No data selling · No ads

No advertising profiles. No behavioral tracking. No brokered personal data. The platform is ad-free by design.

Data infrastructure

Public hazard sources. One civic layer.

Kahu Ola aggregates publicly available open data signals. Text-only attribution — no official logos, no implied endorsement. Live map layers route through /api/ so keys are never exposed to the client.

🔥 Fire & Smoke

NASA FIRMS

Near-real-time satellite fire and hotspot detections (VIIRS/MODIS). Global coverage, <15 min latency for Hawaiʻi.

docs.nasa.gov/firms ↗
NOAA HMS

Satellite smoke and fire analysis for situational context. Smoke plume polygons and density layers.

ospo.noaa.gov/hms ↗
NIFC WFIGS

Official wildfire incidents and perimeter boundaries from the National Interagency Fire Center.

nifc.opendata.arcgis.com ↗
NOAA GOES-West

Rapid geostationary satellite imagery for thermal and smoke context. Visual verification layer.

goes.noaa.gov ↗

🌧 Weather & Flood

NWS API

Official alerts, forecasts, and hazard statements. Red Flag Warnings, Flash Flood Watches, and all NWS CAP products.

api.weather.gov ↗
Synoptic (MesoWest)

Local RAWS station telemetry — wind speed, humidity, temperature, fuel moisture. Critical for fire weather assessment.

synopticdata.com ↗
USGS Water Services

Streamflow and gauge observations for watershed conditions. Flash flood context for Hawaiʻi stream corridors.

waterservices.usgs.gov ↗

😷 Air Quality

EPA AirNow

AQI observations and forecasts for health impacts. PM2.5 and ozone translated into plain civic health guidance.

airnowapi.org ↗

pacioos.hawaii.edu ↗

Tide levels and coastal station measurements. Coastal flooding and shoreline safety context in a simple civic view.
