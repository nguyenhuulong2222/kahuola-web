# Kahu Ola System Ethics Charter

This document defines the ethical principles guiding the design, deployment, and operation of the Kahu Ola hazard intelligence platform.

Kahu Ola exists to support community safety in Hawaiʻi by providing accessible hazard situational awareness.

The platform must always prioritize the well-being of people and the integrity of information.

---

# Mission

Kahu Ola aims to translate complex environmental and hazard data into clear, actionable situational awareness for residents of Hawaiʻi.

The system is designed to help communities understand environmental risks without creating panic or misinformation.

---

# Ethical Principle 1 — Protect Human Safety

The primary ethical responsibility of Kahu Ola is to support human safety.

System design decisions must prioritize:

• clarity of hazard signals  
• reliability of information  
• accessibility for all users  

The platform must never present misleading hazard information.

---

# Ethical Principle 2 — Transparency of Data

Users must always know where hazard data originates.

Every signal displayed by the system must include attribution to its source.

Examples include:

NASA FIRMS  
NOAA  
National Weather Service  
EPA AirNow  
USGS  

Transparency ensures accountability and trust.

---

# Ethical Principle 3 — Honest Uncertainty

Environmental data often includes uncertainty.

Satellite signals may represent:

• wildfires  
• controlled burns  
• industrial heat sources  

The system must clearly communicate that satellite signals are indicators, not confirmations.

Uncertainty must be explained rather than hidden.

---

# Ethical Principle 4 — Do No Harm

The system must avoid causing harm through incorrect or misleading information.

Examples of harm include:

• false evacuation panic  
• misinterpretation of hazard severity  
• suppression of important alerts  

The platform must present information carefully and responsibly.

---

# Ethical Principle 5 — Respect Community Culture

Kahu Ola serves the communities of Hawaiʻi.

The system should reflect and respect the cultural values of the islands.

Guiding value:

E mālama pono.

Care for people, land, and community.

The platform should remain culturally respectful in language and design.

---

# Ethical Principle 6 — Privacy Protection

Kahu Ola does not collect personally identifiable information.

User location may be used temporarily to determine nearby hazards but must never be stored or shared.

The platform must not track users or monetize personal data.

---

# Ethical Principle 7 — Accessibility

Hazard information must be accessible to all members of the community.

The platform must prioritize:

• mobile compatibility  
• simple language  
• clear visual indicators  

Information must remain understandable even for users with limited technical knowledge.

---

# Ethical Principle 8 — Avoid Information Overload

Providing too much technical data can reduce safety.

The system must prioritize essential signals while allowing deeper technical exploration if desired.

Users should be able to understand the hazard situation within seconds.

---

# Ethical Principle 9 — Support Official Authorities

Kahu Ola does not replace official emergency management agencies.

The platform must always link users to authoritative sources including:

HIEMA  
County Emergency Management  
National Weather Service  

Official evacuation orders and instructions must always come from authorities.

---

# Ethical Principle 10 — Accountability

The Kahu Ola team accepts responsibility for maintaining system integrity.

The platform must remain transparent about:

• system limitations  
• data freshness  
• degraded states  

Mistakes must be acknowledged and corrected quickly.

---

# Ethical Design Commitment

Kahu Ola will continue to evolve through feedback from:

• community members  
• emergency professionals  
• scientists  
• civic partners  

The platform must remain accountable to the communities it serves.

---

# Closing Commitment

Technology should strengthen community resilience.

Kahu Ola commits to responsible information systems that support safety, transparency, and trust.

E mālama pono.
