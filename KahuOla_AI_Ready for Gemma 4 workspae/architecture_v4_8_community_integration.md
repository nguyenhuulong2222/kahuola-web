# V4.8 Community Integration
## Civic Fit, Hawaiʻi Context, and Public-Facing Community Design

**Status:** Authoritative V4.8 Community Integration Spec

## Purpose

Kahu Ola is not only a technical platform.
It is a civic platform for Hawaiʻi.

This document defines how Kahu Ola should integrate with community realities, cultural context, and public trust practices across the islands.

## Core Principle

Kahu Ola should feel like a calm public utility, not a sensational disaster product.

It must support:
- residents
- families
- kūpuna
- keiki
- community organizers
- local journalists
- preparedness-minded residents

## Hawaiʻi Civic Fit

Kahu Ola should reflect Hawaiʻi in tone and design through:
- calm wording
- island-aware geography
- local relevance
- visible official Hawaiʻi emergency links
- respectful cultural language where appropriate

## Kūpuna and Keiki Considerations

Recommended patterns:
- clear readable typography
- simple first-layer wording
- smoke and flood notes for sensitive groups
- practical, non-tokenistic community reminders

Example:
Kūpuna, keiki, and residents in low-lying areas should monitor official alerts and avoid unnecessary travel during heavy rain.

## Community Trust Building

Community trust can be strengthened through:
- visible source attribution
- freshness honesty
- official links
- island-specific pages
- calm degraded-state language
- screenshot-friendly signal cards for sharing

## Distribution and Community Use

Kahu Ola should support:
- local news screenshot sharing
- Facebook group sharing
- Nextdoor sharing
- family text / messaging handoff
- mobile-first access

## Final Requirement

All public-facing Kahu Ola surfaces should respect Hawaiʻi community context and preserve civic trust before release.
